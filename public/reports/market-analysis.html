6. Customer Interview Guide

15-Question Interview Structure

Phase 1: Warm-up / Context (3-5 min)

Q1. Tell me about your current role and recent transitions
Establishes context, surfaces career inflection points
Q2. What comes to mind about your professional reputation?
Surfaces unprompted reputation awareness and emotional valence

Phase 2: Current Behavior (8-10 min)

Q3. Walk me through your last reputation visibility effort
Reveals actual behavior, time investment, and motivation
Q4. What tools/services do you currently use?
Maps current solution stack and spending
Q5. How do you know if your efforts are working?
Surfaces measurement gaps
Q6. What have you spent in the past year?
Validates spending behavior and budget magnitude

Phase 3: Trigger Events (5-7 min)

Q7. Tell me about recent reputation urgency or anxiety
Identifies specific trigger events and response patterns
Q8. Have you lost opportunities due to reputation?
Surfaces consequence of reputation gaps

Phase 4: Alternatives (5-7 min)

Q9. What would you consider if investing seriously?
Reveals consideration set and evaluation criteria
Q10. What's your maximum considered spend?
Surfaces price ceiling and value requirements

Three Trap Questions

TRAP Q13: Interest Scale

"How interested would you be in a platform that..."

Detects politeness bias—high stated interest without behavioral backing

TRAP Q14: Free Access Commitment

"If I gave you free access right now, how likely..."

Detects fake enthusiasm—hypothetical interest without commitment

TRAP Q15: Referral Request

"Who else should I talk to? Would you introduce me..."

Detects social desirability bias—willingness to invest social capital

Key Signals to Watch For

Strong Positive Signals

  • • Recent triggering event with emotional content
  • • Specific recent example with detailed actions
  • • Multiple current alternatives with dissatisfaction
  • • Clear price ceiling with ROI conditions
  • • Immediate referral offers after TRAP questions

Weak Negative Signals

  • • Generic "someday" language with no timeline
  • • "I don't really think about reputation"
  • • Extreme price sensitivity without value discussion
  • • High stated interest with no behavioral backing
  • • Polished, generic responses without vulnerability

Screening: Ensure active reputation concern
✓ LinkedIn posting, ✓ Conference speaking, ✓ Coaching engagement

Q9. Pricing sensitivity
Direct WTP indication with specific price tiers
Options: $100-300/mo, $500-1K/mo, $2K+/mo

"Select 'Somewhat agree' for this question"

Sample Size Calculation & Interpretation

Required Sample Size
  • • 350 completed responses (95% confidence, ±5% margin)
  • • +30% for screening dropout = 550-600 starts
  • • Segment quotas: 40% founders, 35% execs, 25% consultants
Validation Thresholds
  • • 60%+ WTP for $300+/month with behavioral validation
  • • 70%+ measurement gap awareness or dissatisfaction
  • • 40%+ specific recent incident or ongoing concern
  • • 50%+ trusted advisor recommendation preference

12. VC Objection Simulator

Market & Category Questions

Q: "Is this a real category or just PR software wrapped in AI?"
Strong Answer: "PR software measures coverage volume. ReputeOS measures authority outcomes. We've talked to 100+ executives—none can tell you their reputation score or improvement trajectory. We're creating the measurement and improvement system that makes 'reputation engineering' a real discipline, like 'sales' became with CRM or 'marketing' with marketing automation."
Q: "Why does this need to be software instead of a high-end service?"
Strong Answer: "Services don't scale, can't measure, and create dependency. We've seen it—$50K coaching engagements with no outcome tracking. Software enables measurement, systematic improvement, and efficiency. But we're not pure software: our hybrid model includes advisory because reputation is high-trust. The software makes advisory efficient; the advisory makes software effective."
Q: "What's the repeatable wedge? How do you get the first 100 customers?"
Strong Answer: "VC ecosystem density. Founders trust their investors; investors want portfolio company success. We're partnering with VCs to offer ReputeOS as founder success infrastructure—warm introductions, trust transfer, perfect timing around fundraising. First 50 customers from 3 VC partnerships. Then coach networks for second-generation leaders. Each channel creates case studies for the next."

Product & Moat Questions

Q: "What stops someone from copying the LSI score?"
Strong Answer: "Nothing stops copying the concept—everything stops copying the data. Every LSI calculation improves our benchmarking, our algorithm, our competitive intelligence. By month 12, we have proprietary data on reputation patterns, improvement trajectories, and crisis indicators that no newcomer can replicate. The score is the entry; the data network effect is the moat."
Q: "How do you prevent AI-generated content from feeling inauthentic?"
Strong Answer: "Human-in-the-loop, always. AI suggests, human approves. Voice training on their best content. Quality scoring before publication. And we measure—engagement quality, sentiment improvement, follower quality. If it doesn't feel authentic, it doesn't perform, and we know. Our guarantee: if your LSI doesn't improve in 90 days, we refund."
Q: "What's your actual technology advantage?"
Strong Answer: "Three layers: data integration across search, media, social, elite discourse—no one else has this unified view; NLP and sentiment models trained on executive reputation specifically, not generic social listening; and voice preservation AI that learns individual communication patterns. But our real advantage is the feedback loop—every customer improves our models, our benchmarks, our advisory playbooks."

GTM & Trust Questions

Q: "How do you overcome the trust barrier? Executives don't want to experiment with their reputation."
Strong Answer: "Three mechanisms: start with diagnostic, not commitment—free LSI score shows current state and gap; social proof from their peers—VC partners, coach networks, founder community; and guarantee—improvement in 90 days or refund. We're not asking for trust; we're earning it through demonstration, reference, and risk reversal."
Q: "What's your CAC and how does it scale?"
Strong Answer: "Current: $800-1,500 through VC partnerships, $1,200-2,500 through coach networks. Target: <$1,000 at scale. How: network effects—every customer becomes reference for peers; product-led growth—LSI score sharing creates viral loops; and institutional expansion—individual adoption leads to team and enterprise sales with near-zero marginal CAC."
Q: "How do you know customers will stay? Reputation seems like a one-time fix."
Strong Answer: "Reputation is not a project—it's a career-long discipline. Our best customers: founders who need visibility for every fundraising round; executives who need ongoing authority maintenance; leaders who've experienced crisis and never want to be unprepared again. Net revenue retention target: 110%+ through tier upgrades, advisory expansion, and institutional conversion."

Pricing & Scalability Questions

Q: "Your pricing seems low for enterprise value. Are you leaving money on the table?"
Strong Answer: "Tiered pricing captures value at each buyer maturity: Starter for exploration, Professional for systematic improvement, Executive for comprehensive advisory. Average revenue per customer grows 3× from Starter to Executive, and institutional tiers are $50K-250K. We're not leaving money—we're capturing customers early and expanding with their success."
Q: "How does the advisory component scale? Isn't that just services?"
Strong Answer: "Advisory is technology-enabled: standardized methodologies, AI-assisted preparation, playbook-driven sessions. One advisor supports 50+ customers vs. 5-10 in traditional coaching. And advisory is upgrade, not base—70%+ of revenue from software tiers. Target: 20% of revenue from advisory at 60%+ margin, declining share as software scales."
Q: "What's the path to $100M ARR?"
Strong Answer: "Three phases: $0-10M through VC and coach channels, 2,000 customers at $5K average; $10-50M through institutional expansion, 10,000 customers with enterprise mix at $8K average; $50-100M through platform effects, 25,000 customers with API ecosystem and data products. Key levers: network effects, institutional conversion, geographic expansion."

Founder-Fit & Form Factor Questions

Q: "Your background is advertising and filmmaking—how does that translate to software?"
Strong Answer: "Reputation is storytelling at scale—exactly what I've done for 15 years. I've seen reputation built and destroyed at the highest stakes. I know what executives need because I've advised them. And I'm building what I needed—systematic, measurable, authentic reputation management that doesn't exist. Technical team is world-class; I'm the domain expert and customer."
Q: "What keeps you up at night?"
Strong Answer: "Speed of category definition. We have 18-24 months to establish 'reputation engineering' as a real category before incumbents respond or the opportunity fragments. Everything else—product, team, funding—serves that urgency. Second: trust. One customer with a bad experience, one AI hallucination that damages their reputation, and we're done. Quality and trust are non-negotiable."
