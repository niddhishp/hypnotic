Executive Summary

Hypnotic represents a paradigm shift in creative workflow management, addressing the fundamental bottleneck in advertising, filmmaking, and content creation: the journey from raw idea to structured, executable creative output.

Market Opportunity

The convergence of AI capabilities with creative workflow automation presents a $12.8B addressable market, with significant whitespace in end-to-end creative ideation and execution platforms.

Strategic Positioning

Positioned between fragmented AI tools and traditional creative software, Hypnotic offers the first comprehensive operating system for creative professionals.

Underappreciated Threats

1. Workflow Inertia

Creative professionals deeply embedded in existing tool ecosystems may resist switching, even for superior functionality.

Mitigation: Focus on integration with existing workflows rather than replacement.

2. AI Model Commoditization

As generative AI models become commoditized, differentiation shifts from raw capabilities to workflow orchestration.

Mitigation: Build proprietary workflow algorithms and creative methodology IP.

3. Enterprise Build vs. Buy

Large agencies and brands may develop in-house solutions tailored to their specific creative processes.

Mitigation: Target mid-market where build cost is prohibitive but budget exists for premium tools.

Customer Interview Guide

Interview Framework

Interview Objectives

  • • Validate workflow pain points
  • • Understand current tool usage and spend
  • • Identify decision-making processes
  • • Test pricing sensitivity
  • • Gather voice-of-customer language

Target Interviewees

  • • Production Studio Heads
  • • Content Strategy Managers

15-Question Interview Guide

Current Workflow & Pain Points

1. Walk me through your last creative project from initial concept to final delivery.
Goal: Understand current workflow complexity and timeline
2. What tools do you currently use for ideation, scripting, and production planning?
Goal: Map current tool ecosystem and integration points
3. Where do you spend the most time in your creative process?
Goal: Identify workflow bottlenecks and time sinks
4. TRAP QUESTION: If you could wave a magic wand and solve one problem in your creative process, what would it be?
Goal: Surface deepest pain points and ideal solutions

Tool Usage & Investment

5. How much does your team currently spend on creative software and tools monthly?
Goal: Establish budget context and pricing anchors
6. Which of your current tools provides the most value? Why?
Goal: Understand value drivers and success metrics
7. What's your biggest frustration with your current tool stack?
Goal: Identify integration and workflow pain points
8. TRAP QUESTION: Have you tried to build any internal tools or workflows to solve these problems?
Goal: Assess DIY mentality and internal resource allocation

Decision-Making Process

9. Walk me through the last time your team adopted a new creative tool.
Goal: Understand purchase decision timeline and stakeholders
10. Who would need to be involved in the decision to adopt a new creative platform?
Goal: Map decision-making unit and approval process
11. What would need to be true for you to justify investing in a new creative tool?
Goal: Identify success criteria and ROI expectations

Solution Validation

12. If a platform could reduce your concept development time by 50%, what would that be worth to you?
Goal: Test value proposition and pricing sensitivity
13. What concerns would you have about using AI in your creative process?
Goal: Surface objections and risk perceptions
14. TRAP QUESTION: Have you ever paid for a creative tool that didn't deliver the expected value?
Goal: Understand risk aversion and past disappointments
15. How would you prefer to evaluate a new creative platform before committing?
Goal: Identify preferred trial methods and success criteria

Key Signals to Watch For

Strong Positive Signals

  • • Specific, recent examples of workflow pain
  • • Quantified time/money waste in current process
  • • Active search for solutions (Google, communities)
  • • Budget authority or clear approval process

Red Flags

  • • Vague or hypothetical pain points
  • • "Nice to have" rather than "need to have" language
  • • No clear budget or decision timeline
  • • Too many internal blockers or approval layers
